Recent Projects

Machine Learning

Understanding the Amazon from Space

Used Keras to train a convolutional neural network (CNN) that does multi-class, multi-label classification of satellite images into different land-use patterns and weather conditions. Explored two approaches to solve the problem - a combination of 2 neural networks(one for predicting weather conditions and another for land use pattern) and a single cumulative CNN for predicting both. The cumulative approach performed better. Obtained an F2 score of 0.9328.

Leukemia Classification Based on Microarray Data

Used DNA microarray data of 1750 samples with 14 sub-types of leukemia. Solved the small-n, large-p dilemma by selecting features using a combination of t-test, p-value thresholding and difference of means. Compared the performance of different classifiers [Naive Bayes', logistic regression, random forest, support vector machines] on the data. Random forest performed the best with an accuracy of 94%.

Generative vs Discriminative ML Models

An important task in the field of archeology is the determination of sex from skeletal remains. The measurements taken from bones tend to be the most reliable way to determine the sex. The performance of a generative models and discriminative models in determining the sex from incomplete osteometric data was compared. The machine learning models under comparison are Naive Bayes, Expectation-Maximization with Gaussian Mixture Models and decision trees. It was seen that Naive Bayes works the best for this problem, which had a limited data set with a large amount of missing data. The results obtained show a higher accuracy than those published by previous studies.

Communication Networks

MANET for D2D Networks

An ad-hoc mobile area network with variable number of base stations and mobile users with free space loss, multipath fading (Rayleigh fading), and interference was simulated in Python. When the base stations fail, a device-to-device(D2D) network is simulated over the existing infrastructure. Security in the form of pre-shared keys is introduced. The probability of forming a D2D connection and the overhead introduced by adding the security layer was calculated using the simulation.

Queueing in Load Balancers: A Comparison Between a Randomized Scheme and a Replication-Based Scheme

Two methods of load balancing – the supermarket model and the replication based model - are analyzed in detail and compared. The supermarket model chooses d servers from N and sends the job to the server with the minimum queue size. The replication based model sends replicas of the same job to d servers and removes the (d-1) replicas from other queues as soon as the job starts being processed by a server. The supermarket model is simulated and compared against the theoretical expected time spent in the system. The results show an improvement over randomized server selections. It was seen that the replication model reduces the overhead in the supermarket model even further.
